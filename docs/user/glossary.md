# Glossary - Key Terms & Definitions

Definitions of key terms used throughout AIExplorer.

## A

### Approval
**Definition**: Formal authorization from a stakeholder or authority to proceed with a project.

**Usage**: "The use case requires approval from the CFO before moving to development."

### Artifact
**Definition**: Any document, file, or deliverable created or used during a project.

**Examples**: Data samples, model documentation, deployment guides, test reports.

## B

### Baseline
**Definition**: The approved project plan that serves as a reference point for measuring progress.

**Usage**: "We established a baseline timeline of 16 weeks in the planning phase."

### Business Analyst
**Definition**: Team member who gathers requirements, analyzes processes, and bridges business and technical teams.

**Role**: Ensures business needs are understood and solution meets those needs.

### Business Case
**Definition**: The justification for a project, including costs, benefits, and expected ROI.

**Components**: Problem statement, solution overview, financial analysis, timeline.

### Business Owner
**Definition**: Executive sponsor with budget authority responsible for project success.

**Responsibilities**: Approves decisions, allocates resources, removes blockers.

## C

### Category
**Definition**: Classification system for AI use cases (e.g., Process Automation, Predictive Analytics).

**Purpose**: Helps organize and filter use cases by type of AI application.

### Change Order
**Definition**: Formal request to modify project scope, timeline, or budget.

**Process**: Requires approval and documentation of impacts.

### Checkpoint
**Definition**: Planned review point to assess progress and make decisions.

**Types**: Weekly reviews, monthly steering committee meetings, milestone completions.

### Closeout
**Definition**: Final phase ending a project with documentation and lessons learned.

**Activities**: Final reporting, team recognition, documentation, knowledge transfer.

## D

### Dashboard
**Definition**: Personalized home screen showing overview of projects and activities.

**Shows**: Your projects, recent activity, metrics, and action items.

### Data Science
**Definition**: Field combining statistics, programming, and domain knowledge to extract insights from data.

**In AIExplorer**: Used to develop and train AI models.

### Data Scientist
**Definition**: Team member who builds and trains AI/ML models using data.

**Expertise**: Statistics, programming, machine learning, domain knowledge.

### Deployment
**Definition**: The process of moving a solution from development/testing to production use.

**Includes**: Installation, configuration, training, go-live, and monitoring setup.

### Dependency
**Definition**: A task or resource that must be completed before another can start.

**Example**: "Data preparation is a dependency for model training."

### Developer
**Definition**: Team member who writes code and builds technical solutions.

**Role**: Implements requirements, maintains code quality, supports deployment.

## E

### Epic
**Definition**: Large body of work that can be broken down into smaller user stories or tasks.

**Scope**: Usually takes multiple sprints or weeks to complete.

### Executive Sponsor
**Definition**: Senior leader providing business case and organizational support for a project.

**Responsibilities**: Approves decisions, allocates budget, removes organizational barriers.

## F

### Feasibility Study
**Definition**: Analysis to determine if a project is technically and financially viable.

**Examines**: Data availability, technical requirements, resource needs, timeline, costs.

### Feedback Loop
**Definition**: Process of gathering input, making changes, and testing again.

**In AI**: Critical for model improvement and stakeholder alignment.

## G

### Gallery
**Definition**: Searchable collection of all submitted AI use cases in the organization.

**Purpose**: Share knowledge, find similar projects, discover best practices.

### Go-Live
**Definition**: The moment when a solution becomes live in production.

**Activities**: System cutover, user activation, support readiness, monitoring initiation.

## H

### Handoff
**Definition**: Transfer of responsibility or deliverables from one person/phase to another.

**Critical Points**: End of phases, completion of milestones, team member transitions.

## I

### Implementation
**Definition**: The execution phase of bringing a solution from design to operational use.

**Includes**: Development, testing, deployment, training, ongoing support.

### Impediment
**Definition**: Something blocking or slowing down project progress.

**Examples**: Missing data, resource constraints, organizational approval needed.

### Initiative
**Definition**: A project or program aimed at achieving specific organizational goals.

**Scope**: Broader than individual use case, may include multiple projects.

### Iteration
**Definition**: A cycle of development, testing, and refinement.

**In AI**: Repeated model training, evaluation, and improvement.

## K

### KPI (Key Performance Indicator)
**Definition**: Measurable value showing how effectively something is achieving business goals.

**For Projects**: On-time delivery, budget adherence, business metrics achievement.

### Knowledge Transfer
**Definition**: Process of sharing expertise and documentation with others.

**Occurs**: During project closeout, team transitions, best practice sharing.

## M

### Machine Learning (ML)
**Definition**: Subset of AI where systems learn patterns from data without explicit programming.

**Applications**: Prediction, classification, anomaly detection, recommendations.

### Metric
**Definition**: Quantifiable measure used to track progress and success.

**Types**: Process metrics (on-time, on-budget), success metrics (business impact).

### Milestone
**Definition**: Significant point or event in project timeline marking completion of major work.

**Examples**: Requirements complete, testing complete, go-live approval.

### Model
**Definition**: Mathematical representation learned from data that makes predictions or decisions.

**In AI**: Trained using historical data, tested on new data, deployed for use.

### MVP (Minimum Viable Product)
**Definition**: Simplest version of a solution with just enough features to achieve core goal.

**Purpose**: Quick validation of concept before full-scale development.

## N

### Notification
**Definition**: Message alert about project updates, deadlines, or actions needed.

**Types**: Email, in-app, SMS (if enabled).

## O

### Objective
**Definition**: High-level goal or desired outcome of a project.

**Example**: "Reduce customer churn by 25% using predictive analytics."

### Outcome
**Definition**: The actual result or impact achieved by a project.

**Measured Against**: Original objectives and success metrics.

### Owner
**Definition**: Person with ultimate responsibility for a project's success.

**Role**: Makes decisions, manages budget, is accountable for results.

## P

### Phase
**Definition**: Major stage in a project lifecycle (Planning, Development, Testing, Deployment, Monitoring).

**Transition**: Formal gate review before moving to next phase.

### Pilot
**Definition**: Small-scale test of a solution with limited scope before full deployment.

**Purpose**: Validate concept, identify issues, gather user feedback before enterprise rollout.

### POC (Proof of Concept)
**Definition**: Small project demonstrating feasibility of a technology or approach.

**Scope**: Limited budget and timeline, focused on proving concept works.

### Predictive Analytics
**Definition**: Using historical data and models to forecast future outcomes.

**Applications**: Demand forecasting, churn prediction, risk assessment.

### Process Automation
**Definition**: Using AI/RPA to automate routine business processes.

**Benefits**: Efficiency, accuracy, cost reduction, faster processing.

### Progress
**Definition**: Extent to which project tasks are complete and goals are being met.

**Tracked**: By milestones, tasks completed, budget spent, timeline adherence.

## Q

### QA (Quality Assurance)
**Definition**: Process of systematically testing to ensure quality and functionality.

**Includes**: Functional testing, performance testing, user acceptance testing.

## R

### RACI
**Definition**: Matrix showing who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for tasks.

**Purpose**: Clarify roles and decision authority.

### Requirement
**Definition**: Specific need or condition that a solution must meet.

**Types**: Functional (what it does), non-functional (how well it performs).

### Review
**Definition**: Formal checkpoint to assess progress, quality, and decisions.

**Types**: Phase reviews, milestone reviews, code reviews, user reviews.

### Risk
**Definition**: Potential issue that could negatively impact project success.

**Management**: Identify, assess probability/impact, develop mitigation plan.

### ROI (Return on Investment)
**Definition**: Financial measure of profit gained relative to money invested.

**Calculated**: (Benefits - Costs) / Costs Ã— 100%

## S

### Scope
**Definition**: Clearly defined boundaries of what is and isn't included in a project.

**Management**: Prevent scope creep, document changes formally.

### Sponsor
**Definition**: Executive providing support, funding, and authority for a project.

**Responsibilities**: Remove blockers, approve decisions, ensure alignment with strategy.

### Stakeholder
**Definition**: Any person affected by or who can affect a project.

**Types**: Sponsor, owner, team member, user, affected department.

### Status Update
**Definition**: Periodic report on project progress, metrics, and issues.

**Frequency**: Weekly team updates, monthly executive updates.

### Success Criteria
**Definition**: Specific, measurable definitions of what constitutes project success.

**Must Be**: SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).

## T

### Task
**Definition**: Discrete unit of work with defined start, duration, and deliverable.

**Assignment**: Assigned to specific team member with deadline.

### Technical Debt
**Definition**: Implied cost of future rework caused by choosing expediency over perfection now.

**Management**: Balance speed to market with code quality and maintainability.

### Testing
**Definition**: Systematic verification that solution meets requirements and works correctly.

**Types**: Unit testing, integration testing, UAT (user acceptance testing).

### Timeboxing
**Definition**: Allocating fixed amount of time to an activity.

**Purpose**: Increase focus, prevent overruns, make progress decisions.

## U

### Use Case
**Definition**: Specific business problem or opportunity that could be solved using AI.

**Components**: Problem, solution approach, success metrics, resource requirements.

### User Acceptance Testing (UAT)
**Definition**: Testing by end users to verify solution meets their needs.

**Importance**: Critical gate before production deployment.

## V

### Validation
**Definition**: Confirming that solution meets business requirements and user needs.

**Different From**: Verification (does it work as designed).

### Variance
**Definition**: Difference between planned and actual performance.

**Examples**: Budget variance (over/under), schedule variance (early/late).

## W

### Workflow
**Definition**: Series of steps or processes that form a complete procedure.

**In AIExplorer**: Use case submission, approval routing, project execution.

## Y

### Yeti (You'll Eventually Think of It)
**Definition**: Planned contingency time built into projects for unknowns.

**Purpose**: Buffer for unforeseen challenges without impacting timeline.
